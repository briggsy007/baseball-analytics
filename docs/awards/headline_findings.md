# Three flagship findings from a 16-model baseball analytics platform

**Author.** Hunter Briggs
**Date.** 2026-04-19
**Companion papers.** `methodology_paper_dpi.md`, `methodology_paper_causal_war.md`, `methodology_paper_pitchgpt.md`

---

This platform comprises sixteen proprietary machine-learning models trained on 7.3 million Statcast pitches from 2015 through 2025, across twenty-five dashboard views, with 784 tests passing at submission time. Three were selected as flagship-award candidates and subjected to an out-of-sample and bulletproofing gauntlet: 2025 holdout evaluation, external-gold-standard comparisons, and — in the contrarian-leaderboard case — a four-comparator base-rate study that separates model signal from tag-filter artifact. All three survived with honest qualifications that the paragraphs below spell out; four other models were retracted, retired, or demoted in the course of the same validation. This is the five-minute synthesis; every headline has a committed-artifact source, and each flagship has a full methodology paper in `docs/awards/`.

## Finding 1 — Defensive Pressing Intensity (DPI): the strongest external-validation result in the platform

DPI is a team-defense metric built from pitch-level expected-out residuals. A `HistGradientBoostingClassifier` expected-out (xOut) classifier trained on 837,571 batted balls from 2015-2022 (holdout AUC 0.894) is frozen, and per-batted-ball residuals — actual out minus model probability of out — are aggregated to the team-season. On 2025, a season the xOut model never saw, **DPI correlates with Statcast Outs Above Average (OAA) at Pearson r = 0.641 (95% CI [0.42, 0.79], n = 30 teams)** — the highest point estimate and narrowest CI of three post-train years (2023 r = 0.58, 2024 r = 0.56). DPI tracks team BABIP-against at r = −0.80 in 2025 versus OAA's r = −0.44; DPI is the tighter BIP-outcome residual metric in the platform because OAA explicitly subtracts positioning credit that a BIP-outcome residual metric, by design, retains. Prospectively, year-N DPI predicts year-(N+1) team BABIP-against better than year-N OAA does — the paired-bootstrap delta in absolute correlation favors DPI by +0.265 (95% CI [+0.02, +0.47], P(Δ > 0) = 0.98 on 60 pooled team-seasons).

The honest limitation: a trivial AR(1) persistence baseline — last year's RA/9 predicting next year's — beats both DPI and OAA on next-year forecasting (AR(1) on RA/9 r = +0.634 vs DPI |r| = 0.46). A combined-predictor regression (DPI + FIP + AR(1)) at n = 60 returns an honest null: DPI's standardized coefficient is −0.061 on RA/9 and +0.116 on BABIP-against, both with CIs spanning zero (p = 0.65 and 0.47). OAA fails the same test at effectively identical non-significance — the null is not DPI-specific. Defensible framing: *DPI is the best BIP-outcome residual measurement at the moment of measurement, not a standalone forecaster*. Natural re-run is October 2026 when 2025→2026 lands and n rises to 90. See `methodology_paper_dpi.md` for the six-gate validation table and §6 addendum.

## Finding 2 — CausalWAR and the Contrarian Leaderboards: a DML-first methodology delivering a front-office-actionable edge

CausalWAR is, to our knowledge, the first at-scale application of Double Machine Learning with Frisch-Waugh-Lovell residualization — the Chernozhukov et al. (2018) gold-standard causal-inference protocol — to Major League Baseball player valuation. A 5-fold cross-fitted `HistGradientBoostingRegressor` nuisance model partials out base-out state, platoon, park, inning, month, and handedness; each player's run-value effect is recovered as the residual-on-residual regression and scaled to WAR. Trained on 1.23M plate appearances from 2015-2022 and tested on a disjoint 2023-2024 held-out cohort (968 qualified players), CausalWAR correlates with real Baseball-Reference WAR at Pearson **r = 0.71 (95% CI [0.66, 0.75])** and Spearman **ρ = 0.62 (95% CI [0.59, 0.68])** — both above pre-registered gates.

The economically interesting product is not the correlation but the disagreement. The **Contrarian Leaderboards** — **Buy-Low** (CausalWAR higher-ranked than bWAR) and **Over-Valued** (bWAR higher-ranked than CausalWAR) — surface the players where the per-plate-appearance causal estimator most disagrees with the public WAR market. On 2025 real bWAR, **Buy-Low hits 68.4% (13/19, 95% CI [0.47, 0.84])**; Over-Valued hits 60.9% (14/23). Across three rolling windows, three mechanism-tagged cores replicate: **RELIEVER LEVERAGE GAP Buy-Low 78% (25/32)**, **PARK FACTOR Over-Valued 70% (30/43)**, **DEFENSE GAP Over-Valued 79% (19/24)**. Named 2024-era Buy-Low picks that delivered in 2025: **Robert Garcia** (2024 bWAR ≈ 0.0, CausalWAR +1.04, 2025 bWAR +0.48), **Randy Rodríguez** (−0.19, CausalWAR +0.36, 2025 bWAR +1.91 — a 2.1-win improvement), **Shelby Miller** (+0.69, CausalWAR +0.51, 2025 bWAR +1.44); **Fernando Cruz** delivered on the 2023→2024 window.

The most interesting part of the bulletproofing story — the one a reviewer should scrutinize — is whether the RELIEVER LEVERAGE GAP edge is CausalWAR's DML signal or an artifact of the tag's own filter (`position==pitcher AND IP < 60` captures a cohort bWAR over-weights on short samples). Four comparator groups were scored across the three windows. The tag-filter universe alone, with no model input, produces a near-chance **56.9%** hit rate (659/1,159, 95% CI [0.54, 0.60]); 1,000 random n = 32 draws from that universe average **56.9%** (CI [0.41, 0.75]). Top-N-by-bWAR within the filter — ERA-leader short-IP relievers, the naive heuristic an opposing analyst would propose — produces **10.0%** (3/30), the opposite direction; high-bWAR short-IP relievers are the *worst* Buy-Low cohort because they regress sharply. CausalWAR sits at 78.1% (25/32, CI [0.63, 0.91]), with no CI overlap against the top-N comparator. The DML residual is doing the selection work — CausalWAR picks low-WAR / callup / bad-luck short-IP relievers, not the ERA-leaders.

The honest limitations: (i) the 2023→2024 window underperformed its WAR-matched naive baseline by 2.8pp on Buy-Low and 8.6pp on Over-Valued, with the miss concentrated in three fringe cohorts (GENUINE EDGE? batters at n ≤ 4, age-33+ relievers, low-WAR Over-Valued picks); (ii) the **mechanism ablation** — masking `inning_bucket` from the DML confounder matrix to test whether a role feature is the causal driver — did *not* collapse the tag hit rates (RELIEVER LEVERAGE GAP +0.3pp, PARK FACTOR +0.4pp, DEFENSE GAP −5.5pp on a small n). The stronger "role features are the load-bearing driver" claim is not warranted by the ablation; selection appears distributed across residual patterns rather than localized in a single feature. Defensible framing — consistent with a causal driver, demonstrably beating any tag-filter-plus-naive-ranker comparator — holds. See `methodology_paper_causal_war.md` §3.4, §3.5, and addenda.

## Finding 3 — PitchGPT: a calibrated decoder-only transformer on Statcast composite tokens

PitchGPT is a 4-layer, 4-head, 128-dimension decoder-only transformer operating on a 2,210-token composite vocabulary that encodes each pitch as a single integer `pitch_type × zone × velocity-bucket`, with a 34-dimensional one-hot situational context (count, outs, runners, handedness, inning bucket, score differential) added at every position. To our knowledge this is the first causal-masked transformer applied to Statcast pitch sequences with integrated situational context and enforced pitcher-disjoint splits. After a 409-pitcher leakage bug in the initial splits was identified and fixed, PitchGPT was evaluated on a strict 2025 holdout of **334 pitchers never seen in training** (zero shared `game_pk` and zero shared `pitcher_id` audited across train/val/holdout) on 53,723 pitch tokens.

On that 2025 holdout PitchGPT is **calibrated to ECE = 0.0098 post-temperature-scaling (T = 1.1201)**, comfortably inside the 0.10 spec gate and near-identical to the 2024 internal-test ECE of 0.0096 at T = 1.1246 — evidence that PitchGPT's calibration is a stable architectural property, not a season-specific artifact. It beats a second-order pitch-type Markov baseline by **56.85% in perplexity (CI [+55.96, +57.64])**, a frequency heuristic by **67.75% (CI [+67.18, +68.29])**, and a freshly-trained 2-layer LSTM with matched inputs and vocabulary by **13.80% (CI [+12.22, +15.51])**.

The honest limitation: the LSTM spec gate required ≥ 15% and the point estimate lands **1.2 percentage points below**. The upper CI reaches 15.51%, the point estimate and lower bound do not, and the gate standard is the point estimate. PitchGPT does beat the LSTM — direction correct, CIs non-overlapping — but the stronger "beats LSTM by spec margin" claim is not supported at this training scale (1,000 games, 5 epochs). A **downstream-utility test** on a 5-class pitch-outcome-bucket target returns a statistical tie: three XGBoost classifiers (PitchGPT + situational, LSTM + situational, situational-only null) land at accuracy 0.359, matching the 35.91% majority-class rate, with all pairwise log-loss and Brier CIs bracketing zero. Restricting to high-confidence pitches reverses the margin in the LSTM's favor. Defensible framing narrows to: *a calibrated transformer sequence model that survives temporal shift and beats naive baselines by wide margins*, not a decision-utility replacement for the LSTM. See `methodology_paper_pitchgpt.md` §3 and §4.

## The contrarian leaderboards edge — a second spotlight

The Contrarian Leaderboards deserve a second spotlight as the most journalist-tweetable finding. The mechanism-tag layer turns each disagreement into a front-office-interpretable story rather than a black-box call: *"this reliever's per-plate-appearance run-prevention residual is an order of magnitude better than his bWAR ledger because bWAR's replacement-level chaining and RA9 sequencing noise depress short-IP relievers."* A GM does not need to internalize "the DML residual at ρ = 0.62 against bWAR" — they already understand *"this list of relievers is underpaid by bWAR; here are four reasons; hit rate is 78% three years running."* That translation layer is the product innovation as much as DML is the research innovation.

## Honest negatives — what we retracted or retired

An award submission that shows only wins is a red flag. Four documented negatives sit on the record from the same validation gauntlet:

- **Viscoelastic Workload (VWR).** A per-pitcher time-decayed workload accumulator, promoted to flagship on a 64-fit seasonal-residual validation showing residual AUC 0.768 on 2015-2016 injured-pitcher data — framed as "calendar-confounding masks buried biomechanical signal." Scale-verified on n = 563 injured fits (2017-2024), residual AUC collapsed to **0.438 (CI [0.425, 0.452])** — below chance. 2025 holdout AUC 0.493, median lead-time 12.5 days against a 30-day gate, delta vs velocity-drop +0.002. **Retracted the same day the scale run landed.** The 0.768 was a small-sample artifact of 2015-2016 label-calendar structure. VWR remains as a descriptive workload tracker and is permanently off the flagship-candidate list.
- **MechanixAE.** A β-VAE over 20-pitch windows of 10D biomechanics features producing a Mechanical Drift Index intended as an injury early-warning signal. First ROC on 30-day pre-injury classification returned **AUC 0.387 — below random**. Demoted to descriptive pitcher-profiling behind an experimental dashboard banner.
- **ChemNet v1 and v2.** A GNN for team chemistry / lineup synergy. v1 r = 0.089; v2, with an opposing-pitcher node and residual objective, r = 0.155. Architecture does not learn this target at this scale. Retired.
- **volatility_surface.** Clean null on a predictability-tax hypothesis: r = −0.013, p = 0.89. Kept as descriptive visualization only.

Sixteen models, three flagships after rigorous testing, four documented negatives where evidence argued against the hypothesis — that ratio is what honest research looks like. The negatives are part of the submission's credibility, not despite the flagship claims but because of them.

## Reproducibility

Every headline number in this document traces to a committed artifact in the repository. The three flagship methodology papers — `docs/awards/methodology_paper_dpi.md`, `docs/awards/methodology_paper_causal_war.md`, `docs/awards/methodology_paper_pitchgpt.md` — are self-contained and reviewable without source code. Each documents its seeds (42 throughout), hyperparameters, cohorts, evaluation windows, checkpoint paths, and the exact bash commands to regenerate every table. Primary artifact directories: `results/defensive_pressing/{2025_validation, prospective_validation, combined_predictor}/`, `results/causal_war/{contrarian_stability, tag_filter_baserate, regression_autopsy_2023_2024, mechanism_ablation}/`, `results/pitchgpt/{2025_holdout, downstream_utility}/`. DPI and CausalWAR reproduce end-to-end in roughly 15-30 minutes on a single-CPU laptop; PitchGPT training requires a consumer GPU (CPU inference works for all three).
